# NIS 2 — Key Articles Reference

> **Source of truth:** Directive (EU) 2022/2555 + the national transposition for the Member State in question.

## Chapter I — General provisions

### Art 2 — Scope

- Entities identified in Annex I (essential) or Annex II (important), generally medium-or-larger size.
- Size-independent application for specified critical types.
- Member States may designate additional entities.

### Art 3 — Classification of entities

- Essential vs important — determines supervisory regime and fine ceilings.

## Chapter II — Coordinated cybersecurity frameworks

### Art 7 — National cybersecurity strategy

- Each Member State adopts a national strategy — context, not direct customer obligation.

### Art 10 — Computer security incident response teams (CSIRTs)

- Designated national CSIRTs receive notifications and provide assistance.

## Chapter IV — Cybersecurity risk-management and reporting obligations

### Art 20 — Governance

- Management body approval, supervision, training, personal accountability.

### Art 21 — Cybersecurity risk-management measures

- The ten measures listed in SKILL.md, "all-hazards", proportionate to risk.

### Art 23 — Reporting obligations

- 24h / 72h / 1mo cadence.

### Art 24 — Use of European cybersecurity certification schemes

- Member States may mandate certified products/services/processes for specific risk categories.

### Art 25 — Standardisation

- Use of European and international standards encouraged.

## Chapter VI — Information sharing

### Art 29 — Voluntary information-sharing arrangements

- Peer sharing of cyber threat information among NIS 2 entities.

### Art 30 — Voluntary notification of significant cyber threats

- Entities may notify near-misses and threats, not only realised incidents.

## Chapter VII — Supervision and enforcement

### Art 32 — Supervisory measures for essential entities

- Proactive supervision: inspections, audits, security scans.

### Art 33 — Supervisory measures for important entities

- Ex-post supervision: triggered by evidence or notification.

### Art 34 — Administrative fines (general conditions)

### Art 35 — Infringements entailing a personal data breach

- Interaction with GDPR supervisory authorities.

### Art 36 — Penalties

- Essential: max ≥ €10m or 2% global turnover.
- Important: max ≥ €7m or 1.4% global turnover.

## Annexes

- **Annex II** — Important sectors (7).
